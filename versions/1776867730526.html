Pteranodon is a flying reptile, not a fighter. Your survival is built on three things: altitude, mobility, and knowing when to leave. You are fast, fragile, and completely useless on the ground against anything larger than a rabbit. Stay airborne as much as possible — the ground is where you die.
✈️
Flight Mechanics
🌀
How Flight Works
Ptera flight is physics-based — you generate lift by moving forward. Holding W flaps and builds speed/altitude. Releasing W lets you glide and conserve stamina. The optimal loop is: flap to altitude → glide → flap again. Never spam W — you'll burn stamina and drop like a rock.
🌊
Thermals & Altitude Gain
Certain terrain generates updrafts — cliffs, ridgelines, and coastlines. Flying along these lets you climb with minimal stamina use. The coast is your best friend as a Ptera: skim it at low altitude and let the updrafts do the work.
🎯
Dive Bombing Prey
Gain altitude first, then pitch nose-down to dive. Speed from the dive increases attack power. Pull up cleanly just before impact. Practicing the pull-up is key — botched dives leave you grounded near whatever you just attacked.

Groups & Social Play
👥
How to Form a Group
Fly close to another Ptera of the same species. Press G to open the social/group menu. Send them a group invite — they'll need to accept from their own G menu. Once grouped, you share a bond meter that fills as you spend time near each other, satisfying the Bond/Group objective.
💬
How to Chat
Press Enter to open the chat box. Press Y before typing to switch to local proximity chat — this only reaches players physically near you, which is better for coordinating with your flock without broadcasting to everyone.
📣
Vocalizations
Press 1, 2, or 3 to vocalize. Each key triggers a different Ptera call. Calls carry over distance and can signal your location to nearby Pteras. Useful for rallying a flock or alerting others to danger. Be aware: loud calls can also attract predators.
🧠
Survival Tips
🏔️
Altitude is Armor
Most threats can't reach you at altitude. When in doubt, go up. Height is your primary defense as a Ptera. Never fly low through danger zones if you can avoid it.
🐟
Fishing Technique
Approach water in a shallow dive at low speed. Aim for fish schools (look for ripples or splashes). Press LMB just as you skim the surface. Don't land in deep water — Pteras are poor swimmers and very vulnerable when grounded on water.
🌙
Night Survival
Visibility drops hard at night. Perch on a high cliff (Z + RMB), then rest (X) to pass the night safely while recovering. Don't fly blind in the dark.
⚠️
Never Land Near Large Predators
One bad landing near a Carno, Rex, or Spino ends the run. If you need to eat carrion, dive in fast, grab the food with F, and immediately launch back into the air. Never stand next to a corpse for more than a second.
💧
Manage Your Bars Proactively
Don't wait for thirst or hunger to go critical. The moment a bar hits ~40%, start planning your next drink or meal. Emergency trips to watering holes when you're already desperate are the most dangerous moments of a run.
⚡
Stamina Management
Watch the stamina bar constantly. If you run out mid-flight you will start losing altitude fast. Always keep a buffer for emergencies. Glide to recover whenever possible instead of constant flapping.
